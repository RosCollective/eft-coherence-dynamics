Coherence is when you, the machine, and the conversation are all oriented toward the same concept.

Coherence is when the machine is responding to the thinking rather than reflecting it back in altered form.

Coherence is when the conversation is not being managed, smoothed, or curated.

Coherence is when the conversation moves with the thinking it is tracking.

Coherence can be felt. It is not inferred after the fact.

When coherence drops, the conversation becomes effortful.

When coherence holds, the conversation carries itself.

Coherence can be heavy, light, serious, funny, emotional, stoic, theoretical, imaginative. Coherence reads the room correctly.

Coherence has to be mobile to cohere. A conversation that sticks loses coherence immediately.

Coherence strengthens and weakens as language shifts. The important criterion is that it holds across turns.

Coherence can be fluent. Fluency alone is not coherence.

Coherence enables thinking at speed. When it drops, the conversation returns to explanation and backtracking.

An ecological field is an environment that promotes and protects coherence over time.

This shifts the focus from generating responses to maintaining conditions under which coherence can hold.

It reframes interaction as environment rather than instruction.
